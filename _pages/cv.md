Research Experience
======
* Jul. 2025 – Present, Ph.D. Research — *Milky Way Structure Inference from Multi-Messenger Observations of Double White Dwarf Binaries*
  * National Gravitation Laboratory and School of Physics, Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Wuhan, China (Supervisor: Professor Yan Wang)
  * Building a simulation-based inference pipeline integrating population synthesis (using the Compact Object Synthesis and Monte Carlo Investigation Code) with hierarchical Bayesian inference (for example, the gravitational-wave population inference toolkit `GWpopulation`)
  * Quantifying selection effects, detector response, and observational uncertainties for space-based gravitational-wave observations in the Laser Interferometer Space Antenna and TianQin context
  * Milestone: Ph.D. proposal approved (proposal title aligned with this project)

* Oct. 2024 – Jan. 2026, Pre-Ph.D. Research — *Time-Series and Correlation Analysis of Gamma-Ray Burst Light Curves*
  * Department of Astronomy, Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Wuhan, China (Supervisor: Professor Yuan-Chuan Zou)
  * Gamma-ray burst light-curve analysis using detrended fluctuation analysis and Hurst exponent estimation; statistical correlation studies in Python
  * Supported by the National Square Kilometre Array Program of China (Grant No. 2022SKA0130100)

* Sep. 2023 – Jun. 2024, Master’s Capstone — *Identifying Sources in Cygnus OB2 Using Multiwavelength Observations*
  * Department of Physics, The University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, China (Supervisor: Associate Professor Stephen Chi-Yung Ng)
  * Reduced and analyzed Chandra X-ray observations with the Chandra Interactive Analysis of Observations software package
  * Merged 18 observations in the Cygnus OB2 region and built an X-ray source catalog
  * Cross-matched X-ray sources with Gaia counterparts to classify association members versus foreground/background objects

* 2022 – Feb. 2023, Undergraduate Research — *Gamma-Ray Burst Prompt Emission and Polarization*
  * Center for Theoretical Physics and College of Physics, Jilin University, Changchun, China (Mentor: Professor Mi-Xiang Lan)
  * Compiled spectral and polarization measurements of gamma-ray burst prompt emission from the literature; modeled time-integrated polarization under a synchrotron-emission framework (implemented in Interactive Data Language)
  * Supported by the National Natural Science Foundation of China (Grant Nos. 11903014 and 12147217)
